Ayam Kecap

This is a staple dish in Indonesia that every home knows how to cook. It requires a little bit of a niche ingredient which is Kecap Manis (sweet soy sauce) but it's a super versatile and delicious sauce to keep at home! It should be easy to find in Asian grocery stores but if you canâ€™t find it, 3 parts honey to 1 part soy sauce will make a great substitute in a pinch. This dish only takes about half an hour to make and is a punch of sticky, savoury goodness. Substitute the meat however you want and replace it with tofu and omit the oyster sauce to make it vegan! Try this for a protein-rich taste of South East Asia!

Ingredients

  • 800 g chicken thighs
  • head of garlic
  • 1 shallot
  • 1 scallion
  • 1 small lime
  • 5 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (Kecap Manis)
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon oyster sauce
  • Water as needed
  • Salt, pepper and sugar to taste
  • Butter to taste

Instructions

  • In a fry pan, heat up a few tablespoons of oil and sear the chicken until evenly browned.
  • While the chicken is browning, dice up your shallots, garlic and scallions.
  • Mix up your sweet soy sauce, worcestershire sauce, ketchup and oyster sauce.
  • Remove the chicken and put in a separate plate
  • In the same pan, fry the garlic and shallots Once fragrant, add in your sauce mixture and let it mingle for 30 seconds
  • Add in about 500 ml of water and the chicken back in the pan and simmer
  • Continue adding water and simmering until chicken is tender
  • Once ready, the sauce should have the texture of a thick syrup!
  • Add in some butter (or not).
  • Squeeze some lime juice and sprinkle some chopped scallions on top and enjoy!
